"What have you done," though softly spoken, tore her apart. The room was silent, but inside her head were spinning sirens. His anxious thumbs and rustling sleeves were louder than artillery. Eventually, timidly, she said,
"I had to."

Whatever he said next drew them both to tears. The ugly kind. Neither would remember it; neither would forget.

He was gone in the hour. Though she had enough pride to match the deep wells in her eyes, she had no shame in calling him repeatedly; no calls connected.
